Overview

Nestled at a lofty altitude of 11,000 feet above sea level, this remote community thrives amidst the rugged mountain terrain. The Dodital expedition is a trek that simply cannot be missed. Renowned as one of the most enchanting treks in North India, it beckons adventurers from far-flung corners, even luring international travelers.

Named after a rare Himalayan fish known as "Dodi" in the local dialect, the Dodital trek is a journey like no other. It commences in Haridwar and winds its way through Uttarkashi, leading you to the quaint and captivating town of Sangam Chati. From there, the path unfolds, beginning with a challenging ascent that eventually guides you to Agoda town.

As you press onward, the trail meanders through a mixed forest perched high above the Asi Ganga river, eventually revealing a hidden valley that cradles the serene Dodital. On the third day of your expedition, a six-hour hike will elevate you to an impressive 11,000 feet. Along the way, the dense woods, adorned with rhododendron, oak, pine, and bamboo, will envelop you in their natural splendor.
